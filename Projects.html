BMS PCB Design

Summer 2016

This summer, as part of my internship at CEiiA, a mobility and transportation company, I designed, built, and tested a BMS PCB design that I made in Eagle. The BMS communicates via SPI to a master microcontroller which handles all of the data. The BMS was capable of over/undervoltage protection, overcurrent and short circuit protection, voltage, current, and temperature monitoring, a State of Charge (SoC) estimate, and much more. It was designed so that each PCB can connect to a 3S to 6S lithium polymer battery pack. The BMS was designed to be modular and scalable, with the main TI BQ76PL536A IC being able to handle up to 192 cells in series.
